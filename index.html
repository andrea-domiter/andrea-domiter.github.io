About Me

Andrea Kayla Domiter

PhD Candidate in Computer Science

University of Cambridge

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Cambridge, working with the Energy & Environment Research Group under the supervision of Professor Srinivasan Keshav. My research focuses on the intersection of machine learning, environmental data science, and urban analytics.

With a strong foundation in data science from UC Berkeley and geographical sciences from the University of Chicago, I bring a unique interdisciplinary perspective to solving complex environmental and urban challenges using cutting-edge computational methods.

My work spans from developing machine learning models for building-level heat risk mapping to creating multimodal forecasting systems for land surface temperature. I am passionate about leveraging data science to create sustainable solutions for urban environments.
